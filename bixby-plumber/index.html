What Makes Bixby's Plumbing Different

Bixby earned its nickname, "The Garden Spot of Oklahoma," from the rich bottomland soil along the Arkansas River — and that same soil creates real challenges underground. Properties closer to the river often sit on sandy, high-water-table ground that stays saturated longer after rain, which accelerates pipe movement and joint separation over time.

Meanwhile, mature trees throughout Bixby's older neighborhoods send roots searching for exactly what a sewer line provides: warm, wet, nutrient-rich conditions. Root intrusion is one of the most common issues we find on camera inspections in Bixby, whether the line underneath is decades-old clay or newer PVC with a single cracked joint.

If your Bixby home was built before the early 1980s and you've never had a camera inspection, it's worth scheduling one — most clay and Orangeburg sewer pipes from that era are well past their intended lifespan.
