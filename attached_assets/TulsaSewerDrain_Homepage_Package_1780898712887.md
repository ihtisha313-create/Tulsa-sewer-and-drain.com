### Our Sewer & Drain Services in Tulsa, OK

#### Drain Cleaning

Grease, hair, soap scum, and years of buildup are the usual culprits in kitchen and bathroom drains. We clear drains of all types — kitchen, bathroom, utility room, and floor drains — using cable machines or hydro jetting depending on what the line needs. Most standard drain cleanings run **$150–$300** depending on the line and method.

#### Sewer Line Repair

A cracked, offset, or collapsed sewer line is more than an inconvenience — it can cause sewage to migrate under your foundation and create a serious health hazard. We locate the break with our camera, then recommend the right fix: spot repair, trenchless relining, or open-cut excavation when the damage is too extensive for other methods.

#### Hydro Jetting

When a snake isn't enough, hydro jetting blasts through years of grease buildup, mineral scale, and compacted debris using water pressure up to 4,000 PSI. It's especially effective on restaurant lines and older Tulsa homes where buildup has accumulated for decades inside the pipe walls.

#### Trenchless Sewer Repair

We repair and replace damaged sewer lines without destroying your yard, driveway, or landscaping. Pipe lining (CIPP) and pipe bursting let us restore your line from just two small access points. It's faster than traditional excavation and typically costs less once you factor in yard restoration.

#### Sewer Camera Inspection

You shouldn't have to guess what's going on underground. Our HD sewer cameras send live footage to a monitor so you can see the exact condition of your lines — cracks, root intrusion, joint offsets, bellying, or buildup. Every inspection comes with a recorded video that's yours to keep.

#### Emergency Plumbing

Sewage backup at 2 AM? Main line blockage on a Sunday? We're on call 24/7 with a **60-minute arrival goal** anywhere in the Tulsa metro. No after-hours surcharges, no call center in another state — a real licensed Oklahoma plumber picks up the phone and gets someone to your door.

#### Tree Root Removal

Tulsa's mature post oak, American elm, and Bradford pear trees are beautiful and notorious for invading sewer lines. We cut and flush roots mechanically, assess the pipe damage with our camera, and for recurring root problems, we recommend trenchless relining to permanently seal out future intrusion.

#### Slab Leak Repair

Many Tulsa homes built in the 1950s and 1960s have water and drain lines running directly under a concrete slab foundation. When those lines crack — often due to soil movement — water migrates under your foundation long before you notice a wet spot on the floor. We locate slab leaks using acoustic detection equipment and repair them with minimal disruption to your home.

#### Leak Detection

Not every leak makes itself obvious. We use acoustic listening devices and pressure testing to find hidden leaks in walls, under slabs, and along buried supply lines — before a slow drip turns into structural water damage.

### Why Tulsa Homes Deal With More Sewer Problems Than Most Cities

If it feels like your neighbors are always dealing with some kind of drain or sewer issue, that's not a coincidence. Tulsa has a specific combination of conditions that puts residential sewer systems under constant stress.

#### Shrink-Swell Clay Soil

The red clay that covers most of the Tulsa metro expands when saturated and contracts sharply during dry spells. That constant movement shifts pipe joints, cracks older clay sewer lines, and creates the offset connections where roots eventually force their way in. If your home was built before 1980 and you've never had a camera inspection, there's a real chance your original clay lines have movement damage you don't know about yet.

#### Aging Pipe Materials

Homes in Midtown Tulsa, the Pearl District, the Cherry Street corridor, and neighborhoods near downtown were built with Orangeburg or vitrified clay sewer pipes — materials that were never engineered to last more than 50 years. Most of those pipes are now 60–80 years old. They're not failing; many already have.

#### Aggressive Tree Root Systems

Oklahoma's native post oak and American elm have root systems that aggressively seek out moisture sources. Sewer lines — warm, wet, and nutrient-rich — are exactly what they're looking for. Root intrusion is the single most common finding on our camera inspections across Tulsa, and it shows up in newer PVC lines almost as often as in older clay ones once a joint develops even a hairline crack.

#### Oklahoma's Freeze-Thaw Cycles

Hard freezes followed by rapid warming put stress on pipe joints throughout winter. When an already-cracked line goes through repeated freeze-thaw cycles, those cracks widen. Winter is consistently our busiest season for emergency sewer calls across the Tulsa metro.

## 4. FAQ SECTION

### Frequently Asked Questions About Sewer & Drain Service in Tulsa

**How much does drain cleaning cost in Tulsa?**
Most standard drain cleanings in Tulsa run between $150 and $300, depending on the drain type, location, and method we use (cable machine vs. hydro jetting). We quote every job upfront before any work starts — no surprise charges at the end. For sewer line work, prices vary based on the length, depth, and repair method, and we'll give you a flat-rate estimate after our camera inspection shows exactly what we're dealing with.

**How quickly can you get to my house in Tulsa?**
Our goal for emergency calls is 60 minutes anywhere in the Tulsa metro. For scheduled service calls, we offer same-day appointments in most cases. When you call, a licensed Oklahoma plumber — not a call center — picks up and gets you on the schedule immediately.

**What's the difference between a drain clog and a sewer line problem?**
A single slow drain is usually a localized clog. When multiple drains in your home are slow or backing up at the same time — or you hear gurgling from a drain you're not using — that typically points to a main sewer line obstruction. Our camera inspection confirms the exact location and cause within minutes.

**Do you charge extra for nights, weekends, or emergency calls?**
No. Our flat-rate pricing applies regardless of when you call. There are no after-hours surcharges, no weekend premiums, and no holiday fees. The price we quote you is the price you pay.

**Is trenchless sewer repair worth it compared to traditional excavation?**
For most Tulsa homeowners, yes. Trenchless methods like pipe lining and pipe bursting restore your line to full function without tearing up your yard, driveway, or landscaping. When you factor in the cost of yard and concrete restoration after open-cut excavation, trenchless often costs less overall and takes significantly less time — usually one day versus two to three.

**How long does a sewer camera inspection take?**
A standard residential sewer camera inspection takes 30–60 minutes. We run the camera through your main line from the cleanout, identify any cracks, root intrusion, bellying, or buildup, and show you the live footage on-site. You receive a copy of the recorded video.

**My home was built in the 1960s. Should I be worried about my sewer line?**
Probably worth a camera inspection, yes. Homes built between roughly 1945 and 1975 in Tulsa were predominantly piped with vitrified clay or Orangeburg sewer lines. Those materials have long exceeded their design life. Many are cracked, offset, or partially collapsed — especially in neighborhoods with large mature trees. An inspection costs a fraction of emergency repair and gives you a clear picture of what you're working with.

**Do you offer a warranty on your sewer and drain work?**
Yes. Every job we complete comes with a written workmanship warranty. If something we repaired or installed fails due to our work, we come back and fix it at no charge. We'll spell out the exact warranty terms in writing before we start.
