### Common Plumbing Issues We See in Kellyville Homes

**Older Homes Near Route 66 and Aging Sewer Lines**

Kellyville's older housing stock, much of it built along and near the historic Route 66 corridor, often still runs on clay or early cast-iron sewer lines. Both materials are prone to cracking and root intrusion over decades, especially with the mature trees common throughout the area. If you're noticing slow drains in more than one fixture at once, that's usually a main line issue, not a single clogged sink — and it's worth a camera inspection before it turns into a backup.

**Septic-to-City Transition Properties**

Because Kellyville sits in a mix of unincorporated Creek County and city-served areas, we regularly work on properties that were originally built with septic systems and later tied into municipal sewer, or that still run septic today. These transition points are a common source of slow drainage and odor complaints, and they need a plumber who knows the difference between a septic problem and a sewer line problem before any work begins.
