## Common Plumbing Problems We See in Jenks Homes

Jenks sits along the Arkansas River, and the city's layout plays a real role in the plumbing problems homeowners run into. In the older, more established sections near downtown and the Riverwalk, mature trees are a frequent cause of sewer line trouble — roots work their way into joints and cracks looking for moisture, and a line that drained fine for years can suddenly back up. **We regularly clear root intrusion from sewer lines in these older parts of town** and use camera inspection to confirm exactly where the damage is before recommending a repair.

In the newer neighborhoods built out toward Jenks West during the city's growth over the past couple of decades, we see fewer root problems but more issues tied to clay-heavy Oklahoma soil — ground that expands and contracts with rainfall and can stress pipe joints over time, especially after a heavy spring. Slab leaks and slow-developing line breaks are common calls in these areas.

Whichever side of town you're on, the fix starts the same way: we run a camera, find the actual cause, and explain it to you in plain language before any work begins.

**Can you plumb your own house in Oklahoma?**
Generally, no — not beyond minor repairs. Oklahoma law requires most plumbing work to be performed by a licensed plumber, and only basic maintenance, like swapping a faucet washer, is typically exempt. Anything involving repiping, water heater installation, or sewer line work usually requires a licensed contractor and a permit. Rules can vary slightly by city, so it's worth checking with Jenks' building department before attempting DIY work — or just give us a call.
