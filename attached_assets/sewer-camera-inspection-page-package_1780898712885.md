### H2: What Is a Sewer Camera Inspection?

A sewer camera inspection — also called a video pipe inspection or sewer scope — uses a flexible rod with a waterproof, high-definition camera mounted at the tip. We feed it into your drain or sewer line through a cleanout access point and watch the live feed on a monitor as the camera travels through the pipe.

The camera transmits real-time footage showing the inside of your pipe in detail — the pipe material, wall condition, any obstructions, and the exact location of any damage. We record the footage so you have documentation of what was found.

It's the most accurate diagnostic tool available for drain and sewer problems, and it's the only way to know for certain what's happening inside your pipes without digging.

---

### H2: What Does the Camera Actually Find?

A sewer camera inspection can identify every major cause of drain and sewer problems:

- **Tree root intrusion** — roots enter through cracked joints or aging pipe material and grow until they block flow completely. Extremely common in Tulsa's mature neighborhoods with large oak and cottonwood trees.
- **Pipe cracks and fractures** — clay pipe (common in Tulsa homes built before 1965) cracks from soil movement and age. The camera shows the exact location and severity.
- **Collapsed or deformed pipe sections** — sections that have lost their round shape or caved in entirely, visible clearly on camera.
- **Offset joints** — pipe sections that have shifted out of alignment due to Tulsa's expansive red clay soil movement. Water still flows but solids catch and accumulate.
- **Grease and scale buildup** — thick deposits coating pipe walls that narrow flow capacity over time, especially in kitchen drain lines.
- **Bellied pipe** — a section that has sagged downward, creating a low point where solids collect and cause recurring backups.
- **Foreign object blockages** — items that shouldn't be in the pipe (wipes, paper products, roots, debris) found exactly where they are.
- **Corrosion** — visible internal rust or deterioration in older cast iron or galvanized lines.

### H2: What We Find in Tulsa Pipes (And Why It Happens Here)

Tulsa has a specific combination of factors that make sewer camera inspections particularly valuable here compared to newer cities:

**Aging clay sewer pipe.** A large percentage of Tulsa homes built between 1920 and 1965 — including most of Midtown, the Pearl District, Brady Heights, Maple Ridge, Kendall-Whittier, and North Tulsa — have original clay sewer lines. Clay pipe has a design life of 50–60 years. Most of it is well past that. When we run a camera through a clay line in one of these neighborhoods, we routinely find hairline cracks, root intrusion at every joint, and sections showing significant deterioration.

**Oklahoma's expansive red clay soil.** Tulsa sits on clay soil that swells when wet and contracts when dry — year after year. That seasonal movement shifts underground pipes, opens joints, and creates the gaps that roots and groundwater exploit. Homes that have never had a sewer problem can develop one silently over years of soil movement.

**Mature oak and cottonwood trees.** If your home sits near large trees — especially oaks — and your sewer line hasn't been inspected in years, there's a meaningful chance roots have found it. We see this constantly in South Tulsa and in older neighborhoods throughout Midtown. Camera inspection tells you whether you're dealing with minor root wisps or a line that's 80% blocked.

## 4. FAQ SECTION

**Q1: How long does a sewer camera inspection take?**
Most residential sewer camera inspections take 45 minutes to 90 minutes from arrival to finish, including setup, the inspection itself, reviewing footage with you, and discussing findings. If we discover something that requires additional assessment or a repair quote, add another 15–20 minutes.

**Q2: Is a sewer scope inspection worth it when buying a home in Tulsa?**
Absolutely — especially for homes built before 1975. A standard home inspection doesn't include the underground sewer line. In Tulsa, where a significant portion of older homes still have original clay sewer pipe, a pre-purchase sewer scope for $150–$250 can reveal problems that would cost $5,000–$15,000 to fix after closing. We've inspected homes that passed standard inspection only to find root-filled or partially collapsed sewer lines. It's one of the best $200 you'll spend before buying a Tulsa home.

**Q3: Will the camera damage my pipes?**
No. The camera rod is flexible and designed to navigate pipe bends without damaging pipe walls. The only scenario where we'd be concerned is a pipe that is already severely compromised — and in that case, we'd note the condition as soon as we see it on screen.

**Q4: Can I get a copy of the camera footage?**
Yes, always. We record every inspection and provide you with the footage. For pre-purchase home inspections, this documentation is particularly valuable — you can share it with your real estate agent, your lender, or use it to negotiate repair credits with the seller.

**Q5: Do I need to do anything to prepare for the inspection?**
Not much. Knowing where your main sewer cleanout is located helps (usually a capped pipe near the foundation or in the yard), but we'll find it if you don't. Avoid running large amounts of water immediately before the inspection — a flowing line can make camera visibility harder.

**Q6: What happens if the camera finds a serious problem?**
We'll show you exactly what we found on screen, explain what it means, and give you a clear explanation of your repair options with upfront pricing. For issues like root intrusion or pipe cracks, we'll discuss whether hydro jetting, trenchless pipe lining, or pipe bursting is the appropriate fix. You decide how to proceed — no pressure.

**Q7: My drain was just snaked and it's clogged again. Do I need a camera?**
Almost certainly yes. Recurring clogs after snaking are a clear sign the snake is clearing a path through a problem but not addressing the underlying cause. The camera will show us whether you have root intrusion growing back, a bellied pipe where solids keep collecting, or a buildup issue that needs hydro jetting to fully clear.

**Q8: Do you offer sewer camera inspection in Broken Arrow, Owasso, and surrounding areas?**
Yes. We serve the entire Tulsa metro area including Broken Arrow, Owasso, Bixby, Sand Springs, Jenks, Sapulpa, Glenpool, Catoosa, and Kellyville. Same-day camera inspections are available throughout our service area.
