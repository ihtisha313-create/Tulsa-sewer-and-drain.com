### Plumbing Problems Common in Broken Arrow Homes

Broken Arrow is one of the fastest-growing cities in Oklahoma, and its housing stock tells two very different plumbing stories depending on where you live.

#### Older Neighborhoods: Clay Pipes & Tree Roots

Homes near the Rose District, along the Lynn Lane corridor, and in established subdivisions east of Garnett Road were mostly built in the 1970s and early 1980s. Many of them still have **original clay sewer lines** — pipes that are now 40 to 50 years old and well past their reliable lifespan.

Clay pipes crack and shift as the soil moves, and Broken Arrow's mature oak and elm trees are relentless about sending roots into every joint and crack they can find. **Tree root intrusion is the leading cause of sewer backups** we see in older Broken Arrow neighborhoods. Left alone, a partial blockage becomes a full collapse.

#### Newer Construction: Hard Water & Slab Issues

Newer homes in south and southeast Broken Arrow — Stone Canyon, Forest Ridge, The Reserve, Traditions — are on PVC lines in better condition. But hard water from Broken Arrow's municipal supply is tough on water heaters and fixtures. Oklahoma's expansive clay soil also causes foundations to shift over time, which puts stress on pipes running beneath slabs. **Slab leaks are more common in this area than most homeowners expect.**

Whatever neighborhood you're in, our team has been in homes like yours — and we know exactly what to look for.
