Common Plumbing Problems We See in Catoosa

Catoosa's mix of older and newer construction creates a specific set of drain and sewer issues. Homes built near the historic Route 66 corridor often still run on clay or cast iron sewer lines, which are decades past their intended lifespan and prone to cracking and root intrusion from mature trees.

Northeast Oklahoma's reddish clay soil expands and contracts with every wet-dry cycle, which is one of the most common causes of shifting foundations and slab leaks we find in the area. And Catoosa's cold winter snaps can crack shallow or poorly insulated pipes, especially in older homes.

Meanwhile, the newer subdivisions growing up around the Verdigris River and the Port of Catoosa sit on lower ground, where heavy spring rain can overwhelm drainage and cause sewer backups. Whatever decade your home was built in, our camera inspections tell us exactly what we're dealing with before we recommend a repair.
