Drain Cleaning in Sand Springs

Slow or completely blocked drains are the most common plumbing call we get in Sand Springs. The cause depends on your home and its age. Kitchen drains clog from cooking grease that cools and coats the pipe wall over months. Bathroom drains collect hair and soap scum just past the P-trap. In older neighborhoods near downtown Sand Springs, aging clay pipes are often the real culprit — tree roots work their way through hairline cracks in the pipe wall and slowly choke off the line.

We clear blockages with professional cable equipment and hydro jetting, and we'll tell you what caused the problem so you're not calling us every few months for the same drain.

A lot of Sand Springs homes were built between the 1940s and 1970s — and many of them are still running on their original clay or cast iron sewer laterals. Hydro Jetting & Tree Root Removal

Sand Springs has some of the most established tree canopies in the Tulsa metro — mature oaks and pecans planted decades ago when these neighborhoods were first developed. Their roots are always following moisture, and the joints in older clay and cast iron sewer pipes are an easy entry point.

If your sewer line keeps backing up after clearing, or a camera shows root intrusion, hydro jetting is the right tool. A high-pressure water jet scours the inside of the pipe clean — removing root mass, grease buildup, and mineral scale — rather than just punching through the clog the way a cable does. We can also mechanically cut and remove active root intrusions and confirm the result on camera before we leave.

Hydro jetting: $350–$600 depending on line length and condition.
