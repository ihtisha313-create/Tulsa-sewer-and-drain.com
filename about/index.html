Why We Specialize in Sewer & Drain Only

Most plumbing companies offer twenty services. Water heaters, faucets, remodels, fixture installs, drain work, sewer work — all of it. That spread is fine for a general handyman. It is not fine when a homeowner in East Tulsa has a sewer line collapsing under a 1960s slab, or when a Midtown property manager has tree roots that have filled a 6-inch clay pipe completely.

We made a decision early: go deep on one thing. Sewer and drain systems in Tulsa have their own set of specific, recurring problems — and solving them well requires dedicated equipment, dedicated training, and a team that has seen every variation of those problems across every Tulsa neighborhood.

  • Clay tile sewer pipes in homes built before 1970 — we know exactly how they fail and how to fix them without excavating your entire yard
  • Oak and elm root intrusion — Tulsa's tree canopy is beautiful. The root systems are aggressive. We clear and line pipes that have been infiltrated year after year
  • Red clay expansive soil — Tulsa's soil swells when wet and shrinks when dry. That movement stresses underground pipes constantly. We diagnose the difference between a blockage and a structural failure
  • Hard water scale — South Tulsa and Jenks area water deposits calcium at a rate that cuts pipe diameter in half over time. Hydro jetting restores full flow where a snake never could

Tulsa-Specific Experience

Tulsa's plumbing infrastructure is unlike what you find in newer cities. Much of Midtown, East Tulsa, North Tulsa, and Brookside was built between the 1930s and 1970s — before PVC became the standard for sewer lines. Those homes were plumbed with vitrified clay tile, cast iron, and in some cases orangeburg pipe. These materials are at or past their design life.

We work in those neighborhoods every week. We know which streets have the oldest infrastructure. We know where the city sewer lateral connections are most prone to root intrusion. We know which soil profiles cause the most joint separation. That local knowledge is not something you get from a company dispatched out of a regional hub in Oklahoma City.
